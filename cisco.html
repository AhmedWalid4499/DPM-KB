Management & Software Platforms

💡

The key distinction: Catalyst Center is a management layer you put on top of traditional IOS-XE hardware you already own — your existing Catalyst switches and 9800 WLCs. Meraki is a completely separate product line where hardware and management are inseparable — you buy Meraki devices and manage them exclusively through Cisco's Meraki cloud. You cannot manage a Meraki switch from Catalyst Center, or a Catalyst switch from the Meraki dashboard. They are two different business models sold to different types of customer.

🧠
On-Premises Management Platform
Catalyst Center
Formerly called DNA Center (DNAC) — renamed in 2023. The single management platform for all Cisco IOS-XE campus and branch infrastructure.

Catalyst Center is a physical or virtual appliance that sits in your data center and manages every Cisco IOS-XE device on your network — Catalyst switches, ISR/ASR routers, Catalyst 9800 WLCs, and Cisco access points. Think of it as the "brain" of a Cisco campus. When engineers mention "DNAC", they almost always mean Catalyst Center — the old name is still widely used in conversations even though Cisco officially renamed it. Understanding Catalyst Center is critical because it transforms how a large Cisco network is operated: instead of SSH-ing into individual switches one by one, a network engineer uses Catalyst Center to push configurations, templates, and firmware to hundreds of devices simultaneously from a single dashboard.

🔍
Network Discovery & Inventory
Catalyst Center automatically discovers all devices on the network using CDP and LLDP. It maps the physical topology — which switch connects to which, which APs are on which switch port. The live inventory shows every device's model, serial, IOS-XE version, and health status. No manual spreadsheet needed.
⚡
Zero-Touch Provisioning (PnP)
New Cisco switches and routers can be pre-staged in Catalyst Center before they physically arrive at a site. When the device is unpacked and powered on, it contacts Catalyst Center (via DHCP option 43 or DNS), authenticates, downloads its configuration, and is fully deployed — with no engineer needed on-site. This is called Plug and Play (PnP).
🗂️
Templates & Config Management
Engineers write configuration templates using Velocity or Jinja2 scripting language. A template might define the standard VLAN layout, QoS policy, spanning tree settings, and AAA config for all branch switches. When a new branch is deployed, you apply the template — the device is fully configured to standard in seconds. No copy-pasting CLIs.
🏗️
SD-Access (Software-Defined Access)
SD-Access is Cisco's Zero Trust campus fabric. Catalyst Center, combined with Cisco ISE (Identity Services Engine), uses VXLAN to create a single logical overlay network across the entire campus. Devices are authenticated via 802.1X — their identity determines their network segment, not which physical port they plug into. SGTs (Security Group Tags) enforce policy. Catalyst Center is the orchestrator that builds and manages this fabric.
📡
Wireless Assurance
Catalyst Center receives telemetry from Catalyst 9800 WLCs and all connected APs — client connection events, roaming events, signal strength, channel utilisation, and error rates. The AI engine analyses this data to detect coverage gaps, AP failures, and roaming issues. It can trace exactly why a specific client's WiFi was slow at 3pm on Tuesday.
🤖
AI Network Analytics & REST API
Machine learning analyses network telemetry to establish baselines and alert on anomalies — before users notice. Catalyst Center also exposes a full REST API (called Intent API) so automation tools like Ansible, Python scripts, and ITSM platforms like ServiceNow can trigger configuration changes, query device status, or open tickets automatically when issues occur.

☁️
Cloud-Managed Networking Platform
Cisco Meraki
A completely separate product line acquired by Cisco in 2012. Every device is managed exclusively via dashboard.meraki.com — no CLI, no on-premises controller.

Meraki is often misunderstood as "Cisco with a cloud dashboard." It is much more than that — it is a fundamentally different networking model. With traditional Cisco, you buy hardware and manage it yourself using IOS-XE, SSH, and optionally Catalyst Center. With Meraki, you buy hardware and a cloud management subscription together — the two are inseparable. Every Meraki device runs a lightweight OS and maintains a permanent tunnel to Meraki's cloud. All configuration lives in Meraki's cloud dashboard (dashboard.meraki.com) — there is no local configuration stored on the device. This means you can configure, monitor, and troubleshoot every device globally from any web browser, anywhere in the world. Zero-touch deployment: claim the device serial in the dashboard, ship it to a remote site, and when it powers on it configures itself automatically. No engineer needed on-site. This is why Meraki is extremely popular for distributed retail chains, multi-site enterprises, and organisations without dedicated IT staff at each location.

🔑
The Licence Model — Critical to Understand
Every Meraki device requires an annual or multi-year cloud management licence (Enterprise or Advanced Security). If the licence expires, the device enters a read-only mode and eventually stops forwarding traffic. This is the most common commercial objection from customers — they are locked into a recurring cost. Orange Business DPMs must understand this risk for contract renewals.
🌐
Auto VPN — One-Click Hub-and-Spoke
Meraki's most popular feature. Every MX security appliance registers with Meraki's cloud. Enable Auto VPN on each MX and they automatically negotiate IPSec tunnels with every other MX in the organisation — full mesh or hub-and-spoke. What takes hours of configuration in traditional IOS is done in under a minute. This is why Meraki dominates the distributed enterprise market.
📍
Location Analytics
Meraki APs passively scan WiFi probe requests from nearby devices (even those not connected) to track footfall patterns. The dashboard shows heatmaps of where people spend time in a building — valuable for retailers measuring in-store customer behaviour. All data is anonymised and aggregated.
🔒
Systems Manager (MDM)
Meraki's built-in Mobile Device Management platform. Enrol company laptops, phones, and tablets into Systems Manager to enforce security policies, push apps, and remotely wipe lost devices — all from the same Meraki dashboard used to manage the network. Tight integration with WiFi: a device not enrolled in MDM can be automatically placed in a guest VLAN.
🏪
Who Buys Meraki?
Meraki is dominant in retail chains (manage 500 stores from one dashboard with no IT staff per store), healthcare (simple compliance, easy guest WiFi), education (intuitive for non-expert IT admins), and SMB. Large enterprise with complex routing requirements often find Meraki's simplified model too restrictive and choose Catalyst + Catalyst Center instead.
⚠️
Limitations Customers Ask About
No traditional CLI (limited SSH debug access available). Advanced BGP/MPLS routing not supported on MX. Full routing flexibility requires Catalyst hardware instead. Meraki dashboard requires internet connectivity for config changes (though traffic still flows if cloud is temporarily unreachable). Licence dependency is the biggest long-term commercial risk.
