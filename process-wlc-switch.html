WLC & Switch Migration Process

Orange Business — Delivery Project Manager end-to-end process guide

🔌 What is a Switch / WLC Migration?

A Switch or WLC migration is the process of replacing old network switches or Wireless LAN Controllers at a customer site with new Cisco Catalyst hardware. Unlike APs, switches are the backbone of the network — every device in the building plugs into a switch. A failed migration means the whole floor or building loses network connectivity. This is why the process has more steps, a formal staging session, a Field Engineer on-site, and stricter change control.

🤔 Why is it more complex than APs?

Switches carry all traffic — phones, PCs, cameras, printers, APs, servers. Replacing one affects everything connected to it. That's why there's a staging session (to pre-configure the switch off-site), a dry run with the customer (to rehearse exactly what happens), and a Field Engineer physically present for the cutover.

✅ What does success look like?

All migrated switches are live and carrying traffic. Customer confirms all phones, PCs, and services are working. DNAC is updated, the GOLD and SALTO orders are closed, and the HOTO document with the runbook and LLD is uploaded to SharePoint and signed off.

⚠️ The most common failure points

1. 3rd party servers not checked during the dry run — servers from vendors like DXC may have special VLAN or routing requirements not in the standard runbook. 2. Staging skipped or rushed — if the new switch isn't pre-configured to match the old one exactly, the cutover will fail.

🗓️ Typical timeline

Pre-migration takes 3–6 weeks — staging sessions, external dry runs with customers, and change window scheduling all take time. Migrations are usually done in maintenance windows (late evening or weekend) to minimise impact. Multi-day migrations require partial migration emails to the customer each day.
